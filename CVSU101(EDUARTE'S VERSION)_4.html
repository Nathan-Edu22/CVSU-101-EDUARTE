CLASSIFICATION



FULL-TIME STUDENT

• One who is registered for formal academic credits.
• Carries the full load for a given semester.


PART-TIME STUDENT

• One who is registered for formal credits.
• Carries less than the full load for a given semester.


TRANSFER STUDENT

• One who comes from another college/university where he started studying for a course.
• One who is registered in the University after fulfilling all requirements as a transfer student.


CURRICULAR CLASSIFICATION

• Shall be based on the actual number of academic units completed as required for a particular curricular year.
• FRESHMAN, SOPHOMORE, JUNIOR, & SENIOR.






SHIFTING TO OTHER PROGRAM



• Students who intend to shift to another program must accomplish a prescribed form for the purpose, to be approved by the Dean of the College where they want to shift to.
• Students pursuing a degree program may be allowed to shift to non-degree programs upon approval of their application for shifting.
• Students pursuing a non-degree program may be allowed to shift to a degree program offered by a college after satisfying the following requirements:
a. GPA of 2.0 or better
b. Submission of approved application form and other supporting documents
c. Passing the required entrance examination

EDUCATION - GPA of 2.25 or better

ECONOMICS / MANAGEMENT - GPA of 2.25 or better






CROSS REGISTRATION



• Should have a written permission from their school registrar to be presented to the CVSU Registrar. The permit shall state the subject(s) and the total number of units the student is allowed to cross-register, and that the University shall be the venue for the course to be registered.
• For courses with prerequisites, they should present an authenticated proof of equivalency of course & description.






LATE REGISTRATION



• The period for late registration shall be seven school days after the regular registration schedule.
• No late registrants will be entertained after this period.
• Undergraduate and non-degree students who register during the authorized period for late registration shall be charged a fine of P100.00.
• No late registration shall be entertained for the summer program.



REFUND OF FEES

• Students who withdraw their registration from the University after a period of not more than 3 weeks or fifteen days of regular classes shall be refundable.


ACADEMIC LOAD

• No student shall be allowed to take more than the maximum credit units per semester.
• A graduating student may be allowed to enroll in more than the maximum credit units, not to exceed 26 units during the last semester.


CLASS ATTENDANCE

• A student who has been absent from classes for at least 2 consecutive meetings must obtain an excuse slip from the Office of Student Affairs.
• Excuses are for time missed only.
• If a student has been absent in 20% of the time scheduled for the class without justification/reasons, he/she shall be dropped from the rolls.


CHANGING OF SECTION AND DROPPING OF COURSES

• Transfer to other courses or to other sections must be made for valid reasons such as: the course is not needed, advised, or there is a conflict in schedule.
• No change in matriculation will be allowed after 3 weeks of regular classes.
• Changes in subjects can be accomplished by filling up a changing/dropping form, duly noted by the Department Chair and the instructor concerned, and approved by the Campus Registrar and Campus Dean.
• A student shall be notified by the University Registrar to drop a course under the following circumstances:
    1.) Registered in a higher course without passing the prerequisite course(s).
    2.) Registered in a major course without passing all the required basic courses, except in cases where the basic courses are offered in a semester concurrent with the major course.


RE-ENROLLMENT OF SUBJECTS

• No student shall be allowed to repeat or re-enroll in a subject for more than 3 times.
• A student who fails a subject 3 times shall be permanently disqualified from further registration in the University.


PRE-REQUISITE SUBJECTS

• A student shall not be allowed to register for an advanced subject without passing/satisfying the prerequisite subject(s) specified in the curriculum.


LEAVE OF ABSENCE

• A student who is granted leave of absence (LOA) must take it within 75% of the time devoted to a semester/term.


HONORABLE DISMISSAL

• Shall be issued by the University Registrar to a student who stopped schooling in the University, provided that he/she was not found guilty of misdemeanor under the University Student’s Norm of Conduct.

REMOVAL/COMPLETION OF GRADES OF “4.0” AND “Inc.”

• A grade of “4.0” and “Inc.” may be removed through one of the following:


1. Without paying the Special Examination Fee

• A grade of “4.0” may be removed within one year by taking the examination during the regular schedule for removal examinations.
• An “Inc.” grade may be removed by completing the requirements for the subject anytime within one (1) year provided the student is currently enrolled.


2. Paying the Special Examination Fee

• A grade of “4.0” may be removed by a special removal examination administered anytime upon payment of a special examination fee of P10.00/unit.






RULES IN SCHOLASTIC DELINQUENCY



Types of Academic Deficiency



1. Warning: Any student who, at the end of the semester, is found to have dropped the course or obtained INC or failing grades, or a combination thereof, in 30% or 50% of the enrolled subjects. Warning status for two consecutive semesters places the student on probationary status in the succeeding semester.


2. Probation: Any student who, at the end of the semester, is found to have dropped the course or obtained INC or grades below “3.0,” or a combination thereof, in 51% or more of the enrolled subjects. The academic load of students shall be limited to 15 units only. If the student incurs probationary status for two consecutive semesters, they will be classified under disqualification status in the next semester.


3. Disqualification: Any student who, at the end of the semester, fails in at least 75% or more of the enrolled subjects shall be ineligible to enroll for one semester. They shall be allowed to carry an academic load of not more than 15 units when they enroll in the succeeding semester.


• A student shall only be allowed two disqualification statuses, after which they shall no longer be eligible to continue their studies in the University.
• Students who have 24 units or fewer academic units remaining in their curriculum will be allowed to re-enroll despite reaching the academic deficiency limits.
• No readmission of disqualified students shall be considered by the Office of the University Registrar without a favorable recommendation from the Readmission Committee.
• The responsibility for evaluating academic deficiencies lies with the students.
• The Office of the Campus Registrar/Department Chair/Registration Advisers shall notify the student of their academic deficiency status at the end of every semester or before enrollment.






TIME LIMIT RULE

• Degree students (4-year course) shall be required to finish their studies within, at most, two (2) years beyond the prescribed number of years.
• Non-degree (2-year course) students shall be required to finish their diplomas within, at most, one year beyond the prescribed number of years in the curriculum.
